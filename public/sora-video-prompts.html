Sora Video Prompts

Master OpenAI Sora with expert-crafted prompts. From cinematic shorts to commercial-grade content, these Sora prompts are tested and ready to copy.

🌀 How to Write Sora Prompts That Work

OpenAI Sora excels at understanding natural language scene descriptions. Here's a step-by-step framework for writing effective Sora prompts:

  1. Define the Scene — Start with setting, environment, atmosphere, time of day
  2. Specify Camera Work — Tracking shots, aerial views, slow pans, close-ups
  3. Add Lighting — Golden hour, neon, studio, natural, dramatic shadows
  4. Choose a Style — Cinematic, documentary, film noir, music video, commercial
  5. Set Parameters — Aspect ratio (16:9), resolution (4K), duration

🔥 Best Sora Prompt Examples

Urban Time-Lapse

Hyperlapse of a bustling Tokyo intersection at night. Camera starts from ground level, smoothly rising to a bird's eye view. Thousands of people crossing in organized chaos. Neon signs blur into streaks of color. Tilt-shift effect makes the scene look miniature. Rain-slicked streets reflect every light source. 16:9, cinematic color grading, 4K.

Dreamlike Nature

Slow-motion underwater footage of bioluminescent jellyfish drifting through a midnight ocean. Ethereal blue and purple light emanates from their translucent bodies. Camera glides between them in a graceful figure-eight path. Tiny particles of light float in the water like stars. Ambient, meditative, IMAX documentary quality. 4K HDR, 16:9.
Sora

Fashion Film

A model walks through a deserted brutalist parking garage. Camera tracks alongside at hip level. She wears an oversized trench coat that flows with each step. Harsh fluorescent overhead lighting creates dramatic shadows. Concrete textures visible in extreme detail. Slow motion, Vogue editorial style, 35mm film grain. Vertical 9:16.
Sora

💡 Sora Prompt Pro Tips

  • Use cinematic terminology — Sora understands terms like "dolly zoom", "tracking shot", "shallow depth of field"
  • Specify the camera lens — "shot on 35mm", "85mm portrait lens", "anamorphic widescreen"
  • Reference real-world styles — "Christopher Nolan style", "Wes Anderson color palette", "Wong Kar-wai mood"
  • Include motion descriptions — "slow motion", "time-lapse", "hyperlapse", "freeze frame"
  • Always state aspect ratio and quality level for consistent results
